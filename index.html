Bomb The Music Industry! is usually described as a music collective and was started by Jeff Rosenstock around 2004. I feel like there's more I could say about them in person, but here's a few words -

The story is that it started out with Jeff leaving a semi-successful band and just saying fuck it and creating an album all by himself at home and putting it up for free on the internet. It eventually evolved into a whole collaboration of friends (including Laura Stevenson, who you'll hear singing in some of these songs). So shows would go from huge whole bands with horns and everything to sometimes just Jeff playing and singing alone to tracks off of his ipod.

They were almost at Fugazi levels of DIY but always stayed real goofy and would tell kids to bring blank shirts to shows so they could spraypaint their logo on them behind the venue. The songs are about being a poor traveling artist in NYC and beyond, the music scene, talkin shit, being real sad, drinking alot, and morreeeee. I'm not sure how well the lyrics aged, but they definitely said and did alot of stuff that was risky for being successful in the 2000's. They also started one of the first record labels that just put everything online and was donation based for digital downloads, years before streaming and all that blew up.

It's hard to say why this band means so much to me. For one, the songs masterfully explore all the different genres and styles of punk rock, and plays with them and makes fun of them at the same time. I think alot of the lyrics helped me explore and discover my depressive tendencies as well, and I'll never forget how much that meant for exploring my brain and putting names on things. They're also just really funny, and kept things fun and exciting.
